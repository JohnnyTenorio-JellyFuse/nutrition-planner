**Why 175–185g protein:** At a calorie deficit with resistance training (MBF), protein at 1g/lb is the threshold for muscle preservation. Below this, the body catabolizes muscle alongside fat — the scale moves but body composition worsens.

## Key Rules

1. **Protein at every meal.** Never go more than 4 hours without protein. 5 meals spaced 3–4 hours apart.
2. **Post-workout shake is non-negotiable.** Always within 30–45 minutes of finishing MBF.
3. **Shrimp or fish at lunch, every day.** The two shrimp recipes hit 42–53g protein in a single meal.
4. **No grains or bread at dinner.** Fish and vegetables only. Carbs eaten at night get stored, not burned.
5. **Salmon twice a week minimum.** EPA + DHA omega-3s reduce workout inflammation and speed MBF recovery.
6. **Egg whites are the hack.** 2 egg whites = 7g protein, 34 calories. Add to any scramble, any meal.
7. **95 oz water daily.** Start with 16 oz at morning wake-up. Dehydration by 2% tanks MBF performance.
8. **Carbs are a morning thing.** Concentrate carbs at Meal 2 (breakfast) and Meal 3 (lunch).

## Omega-3 Priority Note

Wild salmon is the only source of EPA and DHA omega-3s in this plan (the direct, bioavailable form). ALA omega-3s from chia seeds, flaxseeds, hemp seeds, and walnuts are present but convert to EPA/DHA inefficiently. Salmon 2–3 times per week is required to meaningfully support MBF workout recovery and reduce systemic inflammation.

## Goal Feasibility Assessment

- **Daily calorie deficit:** ~900–1,200 cal (1,800 cal eaten vs. ~2,700–3,000 TDEE with MBF)
- **Projected weekly fat loss:** 1.5–2 lbs/week from calorie math alone
- **Week 1 bonus:** Typically 3–5 lbs water weight and glycogen depletion shows up fast
- **60-day projection:** 12–17 lbs is the realistic range with strict adherence
- **15 lb target:** Achievable but requires consistent protein hitting 175g+ every day and no calorie blowouts. If protein drops below target, muscle loss accelerates and metabolic rate slows, which compresses the actual fat loss.
