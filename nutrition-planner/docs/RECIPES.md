### Day 1 — Simple Breakfast
**~30g P · ~430 cal**
*Used on: Day 1*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 3 large eggs
- 1/2 ripe avocado
- 1 medium tomato
- 1 slice sprouted grain bread (Ezekiel 4:9 or Dave's Killer Bread sprouted)
- Extra-virgin olive oil cooking spray (or 1/2 tsp EVOO)
- Sea salt, black pepper, red pepper flakes (pantry)
- Optional: hot sauce

#### Prep Instructions
1. Spray or lightly coat a non-stick pan with olive oil over medium heat.
2. Crack all 3 eggs into the pan. Cook to your preference — sunny side up, over-easy, or scrambled. Season with salt and pepper.
3. While eggs cook, slice the tomato and season lightly with salt.
4. Toast the sprouted grain bread.
5. Slice the avocado half into fans or mash it with a pinch of salt and red pepper flakes.
6. Plate: eggs + avocado + tomato slices alongside the toast.

**Time:** 8 minutes

### Day 2 — Mexican Tofu Scramble
**~30g P · ~450 cal**
*Used on: Day 2*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 4 oz firm tofu (not silken — press and drain)
- 2 egg whites (from 2 eggs, save yolks or discard)
- 1/4 cup canned black beans, rinsed and drained
- 1/2 medium bell pepper (any color), diced
- 1/4 cup fresh salsa (jarred is fine)
- 1 large handful baby spinach
- 1/2 tsp EVOO
- Spices: 1/4 tsp cumin, 1/4 tsp chili powder, 1/4 tsp turmeric (for color), pinch garlic powder
- Sea salt, black pepper
- Optional: hot sauce, fresh cilantro, sliced jalapeño

#### Prep Instructions
1. Press the tofu block between paper towels and a heavy pan for 5 minutes to remove moisture. Crumble into bite-sized pieces.
2. Heat 1/2 tsp EVOO in a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Add the crumbled tofu and cook 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until edges start to brown.
4. Add the bell pepper and cook another 2 minutes.
5. Sprinkle cumin, chili powder, turmeric, and garlic powder over everything. Stir to coat.
6. Push tofu to the side, add egg whites to the empty side of the pan, scramble them, then fold into the tofu mixture.
7. Add black beans and spinach, stir until spinach wilts (about 1 minute).
8. Top with salsa and any optional garnishes.

**Time:** 15 minutes

### Day 3 — Breakfast Salad
**~27g P · ~380 cal**
*Used on: Day 3*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 3 large eggs
- 2 cups mixed greens or baby spinach
- 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1/2 ripe avocado, sliced
- 2 tbsp sunflower seeds (raw or lightly salted)
- 1/2 lemon (for juice)
- 1 tsp EVOO
- Sea salt, cracked black pepper
- Optional: 1 tbsp pumpkin seeds, splash of apple cider vinegar

#### Prep Instructions
1. Hard-boil eggs (or poach if preferred): bring water to a boil, gently lower eggs in, cook 8 minutes for firm yolks. Transfer to ice water for 1 minute, then peel and halve.
2. Build the salad base: mixed greens + cherry tomatoes in a bowl.
3. Whisk together lemon juice and EVOO with a pinch of salt — drizzle over greens.
4. Top with halved eggs, avocado slices, and sunflower seeds.
5. Finish with cracked black pepper.

**Time:** 12 minutes (excluding egg boil — boil eggs night before and refrigerate)

### Day 4 — Pumpkin Pancakes + Hard-Boiled Eggs
**~28g P · ~400 cal**
*Used on: Day 4*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 2/3 cup rolled oats (blended into flour, or use oat flour)
- 1/2 cup canned pure pumpkin (not pumpkin pie filling)
- 2 large eggs (1 used in batter, 1 reserved)
- 1/3 cup unsweetened almond milk
- 1/2 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
- 1/4 tsp ground ginger
- Pinch of sea salt
- Olive oil cooking spray
- **Plus on the side:** 2 hard-boiled eggs (prepared ahead)
- No syrup

#### Prep Instructions
**Batter (make the night before to save time):**
1. If using rolled oats, blend them in a food processor for 30 seconds to make oat flour.
2. In a bowl, whisk together: oat flour, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and salt.
3. In a separate bowl, mix: pumpkin puree, 1 egg, and almond milk until smooth.
4. Combine wet and dry ingredients. Stir until just combined — do not over-mix. Batter will be thick.

**Morning cook:**
1. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-low heat. Spray with olive oil.
2. Pour batter into 4 pancakes (about 3 tbsp each). Cook 2–3 minutes per side until set and edges look dry.
3. Serve the 4 pancakes with 2 hard-boiled eggs on the side. No syrup.

**Ahead prep:** Hard-boil 8 eggs on Sunday. Refrigerate in shell, peel as needed.

**Time:** 20 minutes (or 5 minutes if batter is prepped the night before)

### Day 5 — Fruity Grain Porridge
**~33g P · ~415 cal**
*Used on: Day 5*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 1/2 cup steel-cut oats (or rolled oats for quicker cook)
- 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
- 1/2 cup water
- 3 tbsp hemp seeds
- 1/2 scoop vegan protein powder (vanilla or unflavored)
- 1 tbsp raw walnuts, roughly chopped
- 1/2 cup fresh or frozen blueberries (or mixed berries)
- 1/2 medium apple or pear, diced (or 1/2 banana)
- 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
- Pinch of sea salt
- Optional: 1/4 tsp vanilla extract

#### Prep Instructions
**Slow cooker (Sunday night for Monday + Tuesday):**
1. Combine 1.5 cups steel-cut oats, 3 cups almond milk, 1.5 cups water, cinnamon, salt, and vanilla in a slow cooker. Cook on LOW overnight 6–8 hours.
2. Makes 3 servings. Refrigerate extras in individual containers.

**Per serving, morning reheat:**
1. Scoop 1/3 of the porridge into a bowl. Add a splash of almond milk and microwave 90 seconds, stir.
2. Stir in 1/2 scoop vegan protein powder until fully dissolved.
3. Stir in 3 tbsp hemp seeds and 1 tbsp chopped walnuts.
4. Top with berries and fresh fruit.

**Stovetop (single serving, quick):**
1. Combine 1/2 cup rolled oats, 1 cup almond milk, 1/2 cup water in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer.
2. Cook 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until creamy.
3. Remove from heat, stir in protein powder, hemp seeds.
4. Top with walnuts, berries, and diced fruit.

**Time:** 5–10 minutes morning (if slow cooker prepped); 10 minutes stovetop

### Days 1 & 4 — Shrimp over Squash + Carrot Noodles
**~53g P · ~409 cal**
*Used on: Days 1 and 4*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 9 oz cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined (buy raw or pre-cooked; medium or large size)
- 1 medium zucchini
- 1 medium yellow summer squash
- 2 large carrots
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp EVOO
- 1/2 lemon (for juice + zest)
- 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
- Sea salt, black pepper
- Fresh parsley or basil (small bunch)
- Optional: 1 tbsp capers or Kalamata olives for brine/salt

#### Prep Instructions
**Noodles:**
1. Use a spiralizer, julienne peeler, or box grater to cut zucchini, squash, and carrots into noodle strands. If you don't have a spiralizer, a vegetable peeler makes wide ribbons that work just as well.
2. Lay noodles on paper towels and pat dry — this prevents sogginess.

**Cook:**
1. Heat 1 tsp EVOO in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Add garlic and red pepper flakes. Sauté 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Add carrot noodles (they take longest) and cook 2 minutes.
4. Add zucchini and squash noodles. Toss and cook 2 more minutes — noodles should be tender-crisp, not mushy.
5. If using pre-cooked shrimp: add them to the pan, toss to warm through, 1–2 minutes.
   If using raw shrimp: push noodles to the side, add shrimp, and cook 2 minutes per side until pink.
6. Squeeze lemon juice over everything, add zest, season with salt and pepper.
7. Plate and top with fresh parsley or basil.

**Time:** 15 minutes (plus spiralizing)

**Meal prep note:** You can spiralize vegetables the night before. Store dry in an airtight container. Cook shrimp in bulk on Sunday — 2 lbs at once, keeps 3 days refrigerated.

### Days 2, 3 & 5 — Shrimp + Avocado Lettuce Wraps
**~42g P · ~401 cal**
*Used on: Days 2 (lunch), 3 (dinner), and 5 (lunch)*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 7 oz cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined (medium or large)
- 6 large butter lettuce leaves or romaine hearts (outer leaves work best as cups)
- 1 ripe avocado, sliced or mashed
- 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1/4 red onion, finely diced
- 1 lime (for juice)
- 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)
- 1 jalapeño, thinly sliced (optional)
- Sea salt, black pepper
- Optional add-ins: 1/4 cup black beans (rinsed), radish slices

#### Prep Instructions
1. If shrimp are pre-cooked from meal prep: toss with a squeeze of lime juice, salt, and a touch of olive oil. Ready as-is.
   If cooking fresh: heat 1/2 tsp EVOO in a pan over medium-high heat, cook shrimp 2 minutes per side until pink. Season with salt, pepper, and lime juice. Let cool slightly.
2. Lay lettuce leaves flat on a plate — they act as the wrap shell.
3. Mash or slice the avocado. If mashing, mix with lime juice and a pinch of salt.
4. Fill each leaf with shrimp, a spoonful of avocado, cherry tomatoes, and red onion.
5. Top with cilantro, jalapeño, and a final squeeze of lime.

**Time:** 10 minutes (5 if shrimp are already cooked)

### Day 3 — Mediterranean Tuna Salad
**~38g P · ~455 cal**
*Used on: Day 3 (lunch)*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 2 cans (5 oz each) chunk light tuna in water, drained — use 1 full can + 2 oz from second can (7 oz total)
- 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, halved (about 8–10 olives)
- 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1/2 medium English cucumber, diced
- 2 tbsp red onion, finely diced
- 1 tbsp capers, drained (optional)
- 1/4 cup canned artichoke hearts, roughly chopped (optional)
- 2 cups romaine lettuce or mixed greens

**Dressing:**
- 1.5 tbsp EVOO
- 1.5 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1/2 tsp dried oregano
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder (or 1 small clove, minced)
- Sea salt, cracked black pepper

#### Prep Instructions
1. Drain the tuna. Measure out 7 oz total (one full 5 oz can + 2 oz from a second can).
2. Flake tuna into a mixing bowl. Do not overmix — you want some texture.
3. Add olives, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, capers, and artichoke hearts.
4. In a small bowl, whisk together EVOO, lemon juice, oregano, garlic, salt, and pepper.
5. Pour dressing over the tuna bowl and toss gently to combine.
6. Serve over a bed of romaine or mixed greens.
7. Taste and adjust lemon or salt.

**Time:** 8 minutes (no cooking required)

**Meal prep note:** Mix the salad without dressing. Store in the fridge. Add dressing right before eating to avoid sogginess.

### Days 1 & 5 — Poached Salmon + Vegetables
**~38g P · ~415 cal**
*Used on: Days 1 and 5*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 6.5 oz wild Alaskan salmon fillet (skin-on is fine)
- 1 lemon, sliced + extra for serving
- 3 sprigs fresh dill (or 1/2 tsp dried dill)
- 2 garlic cloves, smashed
- Sea salt, black pepper

**Vegetables (choose 2–3):**
- 1 cup broccoli florets
- 1 cup asparagus spears, woody ends snapped off
- 1 medium zucchini, sliced into half-moons
- 1 cup green beans
- 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes (add at end, no cook needed)

**For finishing:**
- 1 tsp EVOO (drizzle after plating)
- Fresh dill or parsley

#### Prep Instructions
**Poaching the salmon:**
1. Fill a wide skillet or shallow saucepan with 1.5 inches of water. Add lemon slices, garlic, dill, and a generous pinch of salt. Bring to a bare simmer over medium heat (small bubbles, not a boil).
2. Slide the salmon fillet in skin-side down. The water should come about halfway up the fish.
3. Cover and cook 8–10 minutes for a 6 oz fillet at 1-inch thickness, or until the salmon flakes easily with a fork and is opaque throughout.
4. Carefully remove with a spatula. Discard the poaching liquid.

**Vegetables (cook simultaneously):**
1. While salmon poaches, bring a separate small pot of salted water to a boil. Blanch broccoli and green beans 3–4 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp. Drain.
2. For asparagus: simply blanch 2–3 minutes.
3. For zucchini: sauté in a dry hot pan 2 minutes per side.
4. Cherry tomatoes: serve raw alongside.

**Plate:**
1. Arrange vegetables on the plate. Place salmon on top.
2. Squeeze fresh lemon over everything. Drizzle with 1 tsp EVOO.
3. Season with salt and pepper. Garnish with fresh dill.

**Time:** 20 minutes

### Day 2 — Sweet Potato Tofu Scramble + Egg Whites
**~36g P · ~477 cal**
*Used on: Day 2*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 4 oz firm tofu, pressed and crumbled
- 3 egg whites (from 3 eggs)
- 1/2 medium sweet potato (~100g), diced into 1/2-inch cubes
- 1 cup baby spinach or chopped kale
- 1/2 bell pepper, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 tsp EVOO
- Spices: 1/2 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, 1/4 tsp turmeric, pinch cayenne
- Sea salt, black pepper
- Optional: hot sauce, fresh cilantro

#### Prep Instructions
**Roast sweet potato (do this during meal prep or night before):**
1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Toss sweet potato cubes with a tiny spray of olive oil and a pinch of salt. Spread on a sheet pan.
2. Roast 20–25 minutes until tender and edges start to caramelize. Let cool and refrigerate.

**Scramble (morning or evening cook):**
1. Heat 1/2 tsp EVOO in a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Add garlic, cook 30 seconds.
3. Add crumbled tofu. Cook 3–4 minutes until lightly golden.
4. Add bell pepper and cook 2 minutes.
5. Add pre-roasted sweet potato cubes. Stir and cook 2 minutes to heat through.
6. Sprinkle cumin, smoked paprika, turmeric, and cayenne over everything. Toss to coat.
7. Push everything to one side. Pour egg whites into the open side of the pan. Let set 30 seconds, then fold and scramble them gently into the tofu mixture.
8. Add spinach or kale, stir until wilted (about 1 minute).
9. Season with salt, pepper, and hot sauce.

**Time:** 15 minutes (if sweet potato is pre-roasted), 35 minutes if roasting fresh

### Day 4 — Vegan Buddha Bowl + Shrimp
**~37g P · ~470 cal**
*Used on: Day 4*

#### Ingredients (Grocery List)
- 5 oz cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 1/2 can (about 7.5 oz) chickpeas, rinsed and drained (reduce from full can per modification)
- 1/2 cup cooked quinoa
- 1 cup mixed greens or baby spinach
- 1/2 medium sweet potato, cubed and roasted
- 1 cup broccoli florets or cauliflower
- 1/2 medium bell pepper, sliced
- 1/4 ripe avocado, sliced (optional — adds ~80 cal)

**Tahini Dressing:**
- 1 tbsp tahini
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 small garlic clove, minced (or 1/4 tsp garlic powder)
- 1–2 tbsp warm water (to thin)
- Pinch of sea salt, cumin

#### Prep Instructions
**Roast vegetables (meal prep or day-of):**
1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. Toss sweet potato cubes and broccoli (and bell pepper if desired) with olive oil spray and a pinch of salt.
3. Spread on a sheet pan. Roast 20–25 minutes until edges are caramelized. Flip once halfway.

**Roast chickpeas (optional but recommended for texture):**
1. Pat the half-can of chickpeas completely dry with paper towels.
2. Spread on the same sheet pan. Roast at 400°F for 20–25 minutes until crispy.
3. Toss with a pinch of cumin and smoked paprika right after they come out.

**Tahini dressing:**
1. Whisk together tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and warm water until smooth. It should be pourable — add more water a teaspoon at a time if too thick. Season with salt and cumin.

**Build the bowl:**
1. Layer the bowl: mixed greens on the bottom.
2. Add quinoa, roasted vegetables, and chickpeas in sections.
3. Place shrimp on top (warm them in a pan with a touch of olive oil for 1–2 minutes if refrigerated).
4. Fan out avocado slices.
5. Drizzle tahini dressing over everything.

**Time:** 30 minutes (if roasting fresh), 10 minutes (if vegetables and quinoa are pre-cooked from Sunday meal prep)

## MEAL PREP GAME PLAN (Sunday)

**Takes ~60–75 minutes of active cooking:**

1. **Shrimp** — Cook 2 lbs in a hot skillet with olive oil, garlic, lemon. Season. Refrigerate in portions.
2. **Salmon** — Buy 2 fillets. Cook one Sunday night (Day 1 dinner). Save the second raw fillet for Day 5 (cook that day fresh — never more than 1 day ahead).
3. **Hard-boiled eggs** — Boil 8 eggs. Store in shell in the fridge.
4. **Sweet potatoes** — Cube and roast 2 sweet potatoes at 400°F for 25 minutes.
5. **Quinoa** — Cook 2 cups dry quinoa per package directions. Yields ~4 cups cooked.
6. **Grain porridge** — Start in slow cooker Sunday night for Monday and Tuesday breakfasts.
7. **Greens** — Wash and dry kale, spinach, and romaine. Store in bags with a paper towel.
8. **Chickpeas** — If roasting, do it now. Otherwise just drain and rinse and store.
